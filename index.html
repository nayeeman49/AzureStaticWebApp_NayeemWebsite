Future of Outsourcing

A short Article March 24th, 2023

My first topic is the potential of Outsourcing talent in Asia in Tech Start-ups in the UK and America. I personally employ an individual from Bangladesh and have observed the ability of gaining productivity and output, whilst being able to afford to pay an individual in Bangladesh a reasonable wage for their work. There is a huge number of young graduates and like-minded enthusiasts in Bangladesh and the sub-continent region who are passionate to find some form of Technical work, but lack of investment, competition, lack of educational pathways and more, make this hard. This younger demographic can be found as fluent-English speaking and educated who can be hired and trained for a fraction of the cost of UK professionals. £200-£400 a month can be a handsome wage for young Graduates in Dhaka that may be well-educated and experienced professionals can ever be hired. I think we can learn from the approaches taken by India and existing Constulancy/SMEs in Bangladesh.

My Idea to build an online Education Platform

A potenital idea MArch 24th, 2023

My idea to build a new website/App/Social media platform dedicated to aspiring students and professionals to break into IT and Tech. There are numerous pathways, from Bachelors Degrees, Apprenticeships and Certifications to become some form of IT engineer and I want to make that information easy and accessible to the UK and world. Being an infrastructure Engineer, I also have a personal focus on furthering my career by studying certifications, programming languages, learning about potential bootcamps, and networking with people in the TECH industry. I believe there is a large market to capitalise. Once we have build and designed the brand and website pages, an initial challenge for this project is building the source and teaching material. Observing other educational platforms, much audience can be capitalised from courses on Youtube, Udemy, Linkedin and of course internet articles. As our portfoloio of course material develops, more people will be attracted and subscribe to our Youtube channel, Linkedin page and ultimately the website and app platform. Once an Audience is captured, we have the ability to sell course material such as PDF cheat sheets, subscriptions to additional courses and events, etc. A weekly Newsletter users can sign up to is increasingly common. Another potential teaching format would be free Bootcamps. Bootcamps from online websites can be somewhat expensive costing into the Thousands, short bootcamps which involve perhaps a weekly 2-3 hour live online presentation may be able to capture an Audience. I think an even greater potential would be hosting live bootcamps events, there are many Tech events in London, and hosting weekly bootcamp classes could have alot of reach, possibly extending to further networking events.
